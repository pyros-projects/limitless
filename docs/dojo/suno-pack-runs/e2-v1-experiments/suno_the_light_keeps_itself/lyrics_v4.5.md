## Lyrics

[Intro: tape hiss, lone harmonium drone, distant foghorn]

[Verse 1]
Brass and paraffin, four a.m.,
I trim the wick and climb again —
ninety steps I've worn to shine,
every echo answers mine.

[Verse 2]
The letter came on Tuesday's tide,
typed and stamped and dignified:
one relay, one timing wheel —
they asked my hands to teach the steel.

[Chorus]

I wound the spring, I set the wheel,
I taught it everything but how I feel,
and the light keeps itself now.

[Verse 3]
I showed the motor when to turn,
I showed the lamp how slow to burn,
I logged the weather one last time —
fair winds, good glass, all systems fine.

[Chorus]

one long flash, ten seconds of dark.

and the light keeps itself now.

[Bridge: stripped down, harmonium and voice only, almost spoken]
Last page of the logbook reads:
wind south-southwest, visibility good,
lamp in order, keeper —
and I left the rest unwritten, like I should.

[Verse 4]
From the mainland now I watch it sweep,
counting seconds in my sleep —
the dark rolls in, the beam swings true,
it blinks the way I taught it to.

[Chorus]

one long flash, ten seconds of dark.
